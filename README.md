**Who Is Narasimha and What Does the Name Mean?**
The word Narasimha is made from two very simple words.
Nara means human.
Simha means lion.

So Narasimha means a form that is part human and part lion. This form was not chosen randomly. It appeared for a very strong reason, at a time when fear had taken control.

**The Real Story Behind the Narasimha Avatar**
Long ago, there lived a king named Hiranyakashipu. He was very powerful and very proud. He wanted everyone to fear him and obey him. To become unbeatable, he received a special blessing that made him feel no one could ever kill him.
Because of this, he became cruel. He spread fear everywhere.
But his own son, Prahlad, was different. Prahlad believed in Lord Vishnu and was not afraid of his father. No matter how much Hiranyakashipu tried to scare or punish him, Prahlad stayed calm and strong.
When fear, cruelty, and injustice crossed all limits, Lord Vishnu appeared in a form that broke every rule of the blessing. He appeared as Narasimha — not fully human and not fully animal. He appeared at a place that was neither inside nor outside, and at a time that was neither day nor night.
This form destroyed fear and protected the innocent.
This story tells us something very important.
When fear becomes too big, protection appears in an even stronger form.
